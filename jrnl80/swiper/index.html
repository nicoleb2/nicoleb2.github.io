May 5th was "I Love My Park Day" all across New York. The state's Department of Parks, Recreation, and Historic Preservation has been hosting the event for the past seven years.

Communities all across the state were encouraged to help pick up trash and make small repairs at their local parks and beaches.

Event Coordinator Jonathan Cruz had this to say when asked about the goal of I Love My Park Day. "The goal is to make people give back and feel like part of the community, so years later they can say that they helped with certain parts of the park."

Long Island had dozens of events all day at places like Jones Beach, Fire Island, Valley Stream, and Hempstead Lake State Park.

Jones Beach State Park had over 150 volunteers of all ages from around the community come out and help. Of these 150 volunteers a few local boy scout and girl scout troops showed up to do there part.

All volunteers were given a purple t-shirt to commemorate the day, and provided with any equipment they might need to collect trash or paint.
